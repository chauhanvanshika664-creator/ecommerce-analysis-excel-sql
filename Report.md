# 📊 E-commerce Data Analysis Report

## 1. 🧩 Problem Statement
The objective of this analysis is to understand customer behavior, product performance, and revenue distribution using transactional data.

The goal is to generate insights that can help improve:
- Customer retention  
- Product strategy  
- Revenue growth  

---

## 2. 🗂️ Dataset Overview
The dataset consists of three tables:

- Customers: Contains customer details  
- Products: Contains product information including price and category  
- Transactions: Contains purchase records  

These tables are linked using customer_id and product_id.

---

## 3. 🧹 Data Preparation
- Cleaned missing and inconsistent values  
- Verified relationships between tables  
- Ensured proper data types  
- Removed duplicates  

---

## 4. 🔍 Analysis & Findings

### 4.1 Customer Segmentation
Customers were categorized into:
- High Value  
- Medium Value  
- Low Value  

👉 Finding:
A small group of high-value customers contributes significantly to total revenue.

---

### 4.2 Product Performance Analysis
Products were classified into:
- Top Performers  
- Low Performers  
- Dead Products  

👉 Finding:
Some products have very low or zero sales, indicating inefficiencies in the product catalog.

---

### 4.3 Revenue Analysis
- Total revenue calculated  
- Revenue analyzed by category and customer segment  

👉 Finding:
Revenue is concentrated in a few categories and customer groups.

---

### 4.4 Customer Behavior Insights
- Identified one-time vs repeat customers  
- Analyzed purchase frequency  

👉 Finding:
A portion of customers make only a single purchase, indicating potential retention issues.

---

## 5. 📈 Key Insights

- High-value customers are the primary revenue drivers  
- Several products are underperforming or not contributing to revenue  
- Customer retention is a potential area of improvement  
- Revenue is not evenly distributed across all products/categories  

---

## 6. 💡 Business Recommendations

- Focus on retaining high-value customers through targeted strategies  
- Remove or improve low-performing products  
- Improve engagement strategies to convert one-time buyers into repeat customers  
- Invest more in high-performing product categories  

---

## 7. 🚀 Conclusion
This analysis demonstrates how SQL can be used to extract meaningful insights from raw data and support business decision-making.

By combining data analysis with business thinking, organizations can optimize their strategy and improve overall performance.
